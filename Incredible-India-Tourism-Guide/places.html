1. The Golden Triangle (Delhi, Agra, & Jaipur)

If you only have one week, this is the classic introduction.

  • Delhi: A whirlwind of history. Explore the narrow lanes of Old Delhi by rickshaw and stand in awe of the Qutub Minar.
  • Agra: Home to the Taj Mahal. Pro tip: Arrive at sunrise to see the white marble glow pink before the crowds arrive.
  • Jaipur: The "Pink City." Don’t miss the Amber Fort and the intricate, honeycomb-like windows of the Hawa Mahal.

2. Kerala: "God’s Own Country"

For those who want a slower pace, South India is the answer.

  • The Backwaters: Spend a night on a traditional Kettuvallam (houseboat) in Alleppey, drifting past palm trees and quiet villages.
  • Munnar: Endless rolling hills of bright green tea plantations that look like a desktop wallpaper come to life.
  • Best for: Nature lovers, honeymooners, and relaxation.

3. Varanasi: The Spiritual Heart

Varanasi isn't just a city; it’s an experience. It is one of the oldest continually inhabited cities in the world.

  • The Ghats: Walk along the Ganges River at dawn or take a boat ride to witness the Ganga Aarti—a mesmerizing ritual of fire, chants, and bells.
  • The Vibe: Intense, sacred, and deeply humbling. It is the best place to understand the spiritual fabric of India.

4. Goa: Sunshine and Spice

India isn't all temples and forts. Goa offers a laid-back coastal escape with a unique Portuguese heritage.

  • The Beaches: Head to Palolem for quiet coves or Anjuna for a vibrant hippie vibe and flea markets.
  • Old Goa: Visit the stunning 16th-century cathedrals that feel like a slice of Europe in the tropics.

5. Rajasthan: The Land of Kings

Beyond Jaipur, Rajasthan offers the "Desert Safari" experience.

  • Udaipur: Often called the "Venice of the East" for its romantic lakes and floating palaces.
  • Jaisalmer: The "Golden City" where you can ride a camel into the Thar Desert and sleep under a canopy of stars.

6. Amritsar: The Golden Temple

While the Taj Mahal is the most famous building, the Golden Temple (Harmandir Sahib) is often cited by travelers as the most peaceful and moving site in India.

  • Community Spirit: Witness the Langar, a massive community kitchen that feeds over 100,000 people for free every day, regardless of race or religion.

Quick Tips For Your First Trip

  • Best Time to Visit: October to March (when the weather is pleasant and dry).
  • The "Slow" Rule: Don't try to see everything. Pick 2-3 regions and soak them in rather than rushing through 10 cities.
  • Eat Local, Eat Smart: Stick to busy stalls where food is cooked fresh, and always drink bottled or filtered water.
